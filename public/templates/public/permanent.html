Permanent Positions

Finding the perfit fit position is easy with WexLog.

Whether you are seriously looking for a new position, or just considering it as a possibility, or wanting to take that next step in your career and not finding it in your present employer, WexLog allows you to make a facts-based assessment of the market, without high cost and without disclosing your attentions to the world until you are ready. WexLog brings together in an easily-searchable database a broad range of potential employers, whether they are presently looking for someone like you or have not yet reached that stage. When you have decided which potential employers are right for you, you can make the approach through WexLog, to reach the part of the organisation that is most receptive to your offer, without having to climb the organisational ladder, with all its uncertainties. WexLog gives you the information you need to make a targeted, rational approach in a private discussion, and without the formalistic delays often associated with such actions. Even better, your Trust Passport on WexLog, a contemporaneous recording of your work and experience, verified by your employer, your supervisor or a colleague, provides excellent evidence of your work history, avoiding the need for voluminous correspondence with such people who can attest to your skills, something that you may wish to avoid at a sensitive time.

The search for a permanent employee poses risks: finding the right person can also entail telling your competitors what you are looking for, perhaps tipping them off before you are ready to tell the world of your needs. WexLog allows you insight into what is available, the availability of skills that can help you to achieve your staffing objectives. You can make a targeted approach to he people you want, knowing in advance what you will be getting. WexLog takes the guesswork and hope out of recruitment, providing verified facts on which to base a sound decision. Best of all, WexLog permits you to act quickly and effectively, at the lowest possible cost.
