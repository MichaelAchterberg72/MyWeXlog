The Art of Capturing Skills Correctly

Introduction

Skills are important to the MyWeXlog system in that they define the abilities you have developed in your experience over the years. Skills need to be broken down, and captured, into the basic building blocks of the categories that we are all used to. This means that by writing a document you would utilise basic pc, microsoft word, writing, english, as well as the topics that you are writing about. The more in detail you define the skills you utilise the better your portfolio will be.

Capturing Skills

When capturing skills you need to capture all the categories of skills that are appropriate.

Tags must be broken into their base forms. What is meant by this? Lets use cars as an example. If you are a mechanic working on a car and want to tag the experience, how would you do it?

Say you are working on a Toyota Corolla 1.6i GLE. To tag it as "Toyota-Corolla-1.6i-GLE" is not ideal as it would sterilise the experience from people looking for talent with experience in Toyota's. A better way would be to split the tag up: 'Toyota', 'Corolla', '1.6i', 'GLE'.

For example when you are capturing skills for creating the front end of a website you need to include the overriding skill catagories such as ‘Vendor’, 'Project Manager', ‘Project Management', ‘Web Developer’, 'Web Development', 'Front End Development', ‘User Team’, 'Programming', ‘Python’, ‘Java Script’, ‘Django’, ‘CSS’, ‘HTML’, ‘Web Sockets’ and ‘MyWeXlog’, so that when your skills are searched they include the umbrella catagories.

Pilots would be able to capture airstrips / airports / runways they flew into, local rules they dealt with, planes they have flown, airline companies, positions they have flown as such as pilot, co pilot or flight engineer.
